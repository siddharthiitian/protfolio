Hi, I’m Siddharth Prakash — I build machines that try to be intelligent… and occasionally behave better than humans (low bar, but still progress).

I work in Machine Learning and AI, and I enjoy much more than just training models in notebooks. My real interest is building complete systems: understanding messy real-world data, figuring out why the model is failing at 2:13 AM, fixing preprocessing bugs that somehow affect everything, and eventually turning experiments into something that actually runs outside my laptop.

My work spans Machine Learning, Deep Learning, NLP, and graph-based learning methods. I’ve worked with Transformers, Graph Neural Networks, and LLM pipelines, and I’m especially interested in problems where data, reasoning, and human behavior overlap — because humans are basically the most unpredictable dataset ever created.

I approach problems with both a research and engineering mindset. I read papers, attempt to reproduce them, question my life choices halfway through, and then implement practical versions that can be deployed in the real world. For me, success in AI is not just a higher accuracy score — it’s understanding why the model works and being confident it won’t collapse the moment new data appears.

Outside technical work, I enjoy explaining complex ideas in simple language and building tools that make advanced technology accessible. My long-term goal is to develop intelligent systems that are reliable, interpretable, and genuinely useful — not just impressive graphs in a presentation.

In short: I like turning chaotic data into structured knowledge, and structured knowledge into working software.
